## What Is Vampire Crawlers?

Vampire Crawlers is a **turn-based deckbuilder with roguelite elements** from poncle, the creators of Vampire Survivors. Released April 21, 2026 on Steam, Xbox Series S|X, PlayStation 5, and Nintendo Switch.

:::caution[Key Difference]
Vampire Survivors is real-time auto-attack (bullet heaven). Vampire Crawlers is **turn-based** — you play cards one at a time. Strategy > reflexes. If you die in Vampire Crawlers, it's because you built a bad deck, not because you dodged poorly.
:::

## Understanding Cards & Colors

Every card belongs to one of six color-coded types. Recognizing them is the foundation of deckbuilding:

- **<span class="c-red">Attack (Red)</span>** — Deal damage. Your primary tool for clearing encounters. 37 cards, from 0-cost Whip to 5-cost Vandalier (500 damage).
- **<span class="c-blue">Defense (Blue)</span>** — Generate Armor that absorbs damage before it hits your HP. 7 cards. _Pummarola_ and _Pummadora_ also provide post-encounter healing.
- **<span class="c-yellow">Stat Boost (Yellow)</span>** — Temporary stat increases. Some are **Destroy-type** (one use per battle, like _Crown_ for XP growth). 18 cards.
- **<span class="c-purple">Mana (Purple)</span>** — Generate the mana you need to play other cards. 6 cards. _Empty Tome_ (0 cost, +1 mana) is excellent.
- **<span class="c-teal">Character (Teal)</span>** — 22 unique cards tied to characters. Each has a play effect and an ongoing trigger.
- **<span style="color:#ccc">Wild (White)</span>** — Cost `W` (any mana color). _Wings_ reduces next card cost by 1. _Bracelet_ deals 100 damage.

:::tip[Color synergy matters]
Characters trigger effects based on specific colors you play. Antonio grants +10% damage when you play a **<span class="c-red">red</span>** card. Build around your leading character's trigger color.
:::

## Combat Basics

Each dungeon floor has enemy encounters — beat all enemies to advance. Key mechanics:

- **Mana** — Resource for playing cards. Generated by purple Mana cards. Unspent mana is lost at turn end.
- **Armor** — Blue shield that absorbs damage. Applied before HP damage. Stack it early in tough fights.
- **Hand Size** — Cards you can hold at once. _Bracer_ and _Poppea_ increase it. The bigger your hand, the more options per turn.
- **Combo Multiplier** — Playing cards in ascending mana cost order builds a combo. Higher combo = more damage. The _Combo Stack_ relic is essential for this.
- **Evolutions** — Certain cards evolve when paired with specific relics or conditions. Check the Grim Grimoire (from Library West Wing) to see discovered combos.
- **Disarm** — _Garlic_ and _Soul Eater_ can disarm enemies, preventing their next attack.
- **Freeze** — _Clock Lancet_ freezes enemies for one turn.

:::note[Combo Formula]
Combo Damage = Base Damage × (1 + Combo Stack Bonus × Combo Count)
:::

## The Village Hub

Between dungeon runs, you return to the Village. As you collect relics, more locations unlock:

### 🏨  Gorton Bell Inn
Buy and equip characters (up to 5). Your **leading character** adds their stats + 4 cards to your deck. Additional characters add 1 card each.

### 🔨  Blacksmith
Socket gems into cards for permanent stat bonuses. Unlocked by the **Stardust Anvil** relic. Customize gem drops with **Lapidary Loupe**.

### 🔮  Fortune Teller
Arcana system — powerful run-wide passive bonuses. Unlocked by the **Polentir** relic from Furious Forest.

### 🏛️  Museum
View and toggle collected relics. Available from the start. Relics can be turned off if you want a harder challenge.

## Deckbuilding 101

**The golden rule:** a lean, focused deck beats a bloated one every time.

1. **Mana curve.** Have enough 0-1 cost cards to start combos. Don't fill your deck with 4-5 cost finishers you can't afford to play.
2. **Synergy over power.** A 0-cost Whip (8 damage, multi-target) that triggers your character's effect is better than a 5-cost card you can't combo into.
3. **Trim when you can.** Some events let you remove cards. Remove cards that don't synergize with your build.
4. **Start small, scale up.** Early encounters should be used to set up your engine (mana, hand size, stat boosts), then unleash big damage in later turns.
5. **One defense card is enough.** Don't load up on Blue cards. One good Armor generator + healing is usually sufficient.

## Early Game Walkthrough

1. **Clear Mad Forest.** It's the only starting dungeon. Explores basic mechanics. Find the _Gem Hammer_ relic (break crates/barrels).
2. **Reach Level 10 in Mad Forest.** Even if you die immediately after, you unlock **Inlaid Library**. Level unlocks are permanent.
3. **Collect Combo Stack.** The tutorial gives it automatically. This relic is _the_ core scaling mechanic. Never unequip it.
4. **Buy characters at the Inn.** More characters = more starter deck cards = more options in early encounters.
5. **Run Inlaid Library.** Reach Level 15 here to unlock **Teeny Bridge** (which gives Stardust Anvil + Overkill — two essential relics).
6. **Branch out.** Once you have Combo Stack, Gem Hammer, Stardust Anvil, and Overkill, you can tackle any dungeon chain.

## Quick Tips & Tricks

:::tip[Level thresholds unlock dungeons]
You don't need to win — just hit Level 10 or 15. Die immediately after, the unlock still counts.
:::

:::danger[Don't skip relics]
Relics are permanent and can be toggled but are always available. Hidden in breakable objects (barrels, crates, torches). The _Guiding Light_ relic shows destructibles on the minimap.
:::

:::note[Disco Mode]
After buying 5+ characters at the Inn, there's a 10% chance per room that Disco Mode triggers — granting a 5% discount on all purchases for that visit.
:::

- Gems (unlocked by _Gem Hammer_) add permanent bonuses to individual cards at the Blacksmith
- Arcanas (unlocked by _Polentir_) provide run-wide passive bonuses — selected before each run
- The _Overkill_ relic lets you keep attacking bosses after defeat for bonus gold (cap increases with upgrades)
- Break _everything_ — relics, gold, and gems are often hidden in destructible objects
